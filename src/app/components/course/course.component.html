1. What is a stock?

A stock is a piece of a company. If you own a stock, then you own a piece of that company. The more stocks you buy, the larger the percentage is your ownage of that company.

Example: Let's say for a company called "XYZ" there exist a total of 100 stocks. If you own 7 stocks, then you own a total of 7% of company XYZ.

Let's visualize this with money. Take the example above and assume that each stock is worth $50. How much would be your total net worth of the 7 shares you own?

$350.

2. Market Capitalization

Let's say the shares of Microsoft are trading at $300 and the shares of Tesla are trading at $900. Which company do you think is worth more?

Answer: Without any further information, you wouldn't know. You might've guessed Tesla; however, what you are seeing is the price of a single share. You don't know the total amount of shares that exist for each of those companies.

What we're trying to calculate is the market capitalization of both companies. The calculation is simple, you just multiply the share price with the total amount of share for that company.

Example: Microsoft has approximately a total amount of 7,500,000,000 outstanding shares as of 2022. Now imagine that each share costs $300. To calculate the market capitalization of Microsoft (total worth of the company), then you do:

7,500,000,000 x $300 = $2.25 Billion

Let's do the same for Tesla. They have approximately a total amount of 1,000,000,000 outstanding shares as of 2022. The market capitalization of Tesla would be:

1,000,000,000 x $900 = $900 Million

As you see, even if the single stock price of Tesla is higher than the one of Microsoft, it doesn't mean that the company is worth more. If you need a visualization of this, then enter the stock price and the amount of outstanding shares in the graph below.

3. How do I trade?

If you just want to trade stocks right now, then you need a brokerage account. Well, how do you get one? It's simple, just google something like "Most popular broker in the US". If you do not live in the US, then just simply replace it with your country.

Important: You need to evaluate the best broker for yourself. Do not just blindly open an account on the first result, you also need to compare fees, security, whether a bank license exists... After all, it's a company you're trusting your money and stocks with for a long time.

Once you have a brokerage account, you have to deposit money. This might take some time. Once your money has been successfully deposited, you can start to trade. Well... maybe? It depends whether the stock market in your country is open or closed. Generally speaking, exchanges (a place where you can trade stocks) are closed during weekends and on public holidays. Also, exchanges are open for a limited time during weekdays.

Example: The New York Stock Exchange is open from 9:30a.m. to 4:00p.m. ET. during weekdays.

4. How does an exchange work?

An exchange works by bringing buyers and sellers together, acting as a middleman.
